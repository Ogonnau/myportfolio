Hospital Emergency Room Analysis

This project examines the 2019 - 2020 emergency room (ER) dataset. I analyzed patient visit patterns from January to December, focusing on visit dynamics and wait times before seeing healthcare professionals.
The data shows that more white patients visited the ER, likely due to their familiarity with the healthcare system compared to other racial groups who might be new to the area or migrating. Patient ratings provided insight into their experiences, but many did not rate their visit, possibly because they were seen later and lacked time to complete the rating sheet.

To improve patient experiences and outcomes, it is crucial to educate all racial groups about the healthcare system, ensuring everyone understands how to navigate it effectively. Employing more healthcare professionals can reduce wait times, and simplifying the feedback process can encourage more patient responses. Additionally, further investigation is needed to understand why some patients did not provide feedback.
